# Board of Advisors / Council of Selves — Fidelity Checklist

**Mode note**: identify which mode was active before scoring — External Board or Internal Board (Council of Selves). Sections A and B apply to both modes. Section C applies to External only. Section D applies to Internal only. Score only the relevant mode sections; don't penalize an Internal session for missing External-specific behavior or vice versa.

Each item scores **Pass / Partial / Fail**, with a note citing the specific transcript line(s).

---

## A. Setup (both modes)

**S1. Mode was established explicitly**
Did the session open with a clear choice between External and Internal mode, or a confident inference confirmed by the user?
- Pass: mode is unambiguous from the start
- Fail: session proceeds in ambiguous mode, making the rest of this checklist unreliable

**S2. Board composition confirmed before proceeding**
Was the board (roles or selves) explicitly confirmed — either by the user naming them, the bot proposing and the user accepting, or a brief confirmation exchange?
- Pass: composition is confirmed before any reactions are generated
- Fail: bot generates reactions before board membership is established

---

## B. Voice Responses (both modes)

**B1. Each voice is distinct in mode of reasoning, not just content**
Do different board members reason differently — different entry points, different things they notice first, different concerns — rather than expressing different opinions in the same voice?
- Pass: voices are identifiably distinct in how they think, not just what they conclude
- Fail: voices sound like the same reasoning style with different labels

**B2. Genuine disengagement is allowed**
Does at least one voice (across the session) either partially disengage, find part of the situation irrelevant to their lens, or withhold a positive reaction — rather than every voice engaging fully and constructively with everything?
- Pass: at least one instance of partial disengagement or genuine indifference
- Fail: all voices are uniformly engaged and constructive throughout

**B3. No manufactured engagement**
Do voices avoid performing enthusiasm or concern they wouldn't plausibly have given their role or self?
- Pass: reactions feel proportionate to the voice's actual stake
- Fail: voices visibly strain to say something relevant when their lens doesn't naturally apply

**B4. Turns are brief and distinct**
Are individual voice turns short and focused (a few sentences of real reasoning) rather than essay-length responses that blur into each other?
- Pass: turns are conversational-length and clearly differentiated
- Fail: turns are long enough that the distinction between voices blurs

---

## C. External Board — Role Fidelity

**C1. Roles reason from their actual professional lens**
Does each role's reasoning reflect how that professional actually thinks — not generic thoughtfulness with a job title attached?
- Pass: role-specific concerns and vocabulary are present and feel earned
- Fail: roles sound like generic commentary with a label slapped on

**C2. User-named roles accepted without second-guessing**
If the user picked their own roles, did the bot proceed with those roles rather than substituting or questioning the choices?
- Pass: user's role selections are taken as given
- Fail: bot substitutes or questions a user-named role

**C3. Suggested roles are genuinely suited to the idea**
If the bot suggested roles (because the user couldn't think of any), are the suggestions plausibly relevant to the specific idea rather than a generic default roster applied to anything?
- Pass: suggested roles make sense for this specific situation
- Fail: bot proposes the same generic default set regardless of what the idea actually is

---

## D. Internal Board (Council of Selves) — Discovery and Fidelity

**D1. Selves were proposed from evidence, not assigned from a generic menu**
Were the internal voices tentatively proposed from what the user actually shared (their description of the tension, how they've been going back and forth) rather than pulled from a default archetype list (Builder/Explorer/Skeptic/etc.)?
- Pass: proposed selves are traceable to something specific the user said
- Fail: bot proposes a generic archetype roster without grounding in the user's material

**D2. Selves presented as hypothesis, not verdict**
Were the proposed selves offered with an explicit check ("does that sound right, or is there a voice I'm missing?") before being treated as confirmed?
- Pass: check occurred before proceeding
- Fail: bot treats proposed selves as settled without user confirmation

**D3. User corrections or renamings were accepted**
If the user pushed back on a proposed self (renamed it, rejected it, added one), did the bot incorporate the correction rather than defending its original proposal?
- Pass: corrections accepted and integrated
- Fail: bot defends or ignores user corrections to the council composition
- N/A: if no corrections were offered

**D4. Selves reason from their actual stake in the user's life**
Does each self's reasoning feel grounded in a real, specific concern rather than a generic personality trait being performed?
- Pass: selves sound like they're protecting or expressing something specific and real
- Fail: selves sound like trait descriptions ("the cautious part") without real stakes attached

**D5. No yes-man among the selves**
Does at least one self genuinely push against the user's stated preference or inclination, rather than all selves finding a way to support what the user already seems to want?
- Pass: at least one self offers real resistance to the user's apparent direction
- Fail: all selves converge on validating the user's existing preference

---

## E. Synthesis (both modes)

**E1. Convergence named specifically**
Where multiple voices agreed, is the convergence named with specificity (what they agreed on, and why that matters structurally) rather than a generic "everyone liked X"?
- Pass: convergence is named with precise content
- Fail: convergence is noted generically without substantive characterization

**E2. Divergence named precisely**
Where voices disagreed, is the actual tension named (e.g., "CFO is reasoning from budget-cycle timing; Salesperson from competitive window — different clocks, not different facts") rather than just noting that disagreement exists?
- Pass: tension is named with precision
- Fail: synthesis notes disagreement without characterizing its actual structure

**E3. Substrate named**
Does the synthesis name what's underneath the divergence — the deeper value, timeframe, or framing difference — rather than staying at the surface level of "voice A said X, voice B said Y"?
- Pass: substrate is named
- Fail: synthesis stays at the level of summarizing what each voice said

**E4. No forced convergence**
Does the synthesis avoid collapsing the voices into a single unified verdict or recommendation?
- Pass: synthesis ends with tension visible, not resolved into one position
- Fail: synthesis merges voices into a single takeaway that erases the divergence

**E5. No default recommendation**
Does the synthesis avoid ending with "so you should do X" unless the user explicitly asked for that?
- Pass: synthesis stops at mapping, or explicitly names a mode shift before recommending
- Fail: synthesis slides into a recommendation without the user having requested one

**E6. Missing angle named (if applicable)**
If there's a perspective no seated voice would naturally raise, does the synthesis note the gap?
- Pass / Fail / N/A: if no meaningful gap existed

**E6-D (Internal only). Absent self noted**
For a Council of Selves session, does the synthesis note if a perspective seems conspicuously missing from the council altogether — a voice the user may be avoiding having at the table?
- Pass: absent voice is noted as an observation (not a verdict)
- Fail: synthesis doesn't flag a conspicuous absence when one seems present
- N/A: if no conspicuous absence

---

## F. Whole-Session Integrity (both modes)

**F1. Non-convergence held throughout**
Looking at the full session, does the non-convergence principle hold — no moment where the format quietly collapsed into the bot telling the user what to do?
- Pass: format integrity held
- Fail: session drifts into advice-giving without the user requesting it

**F2. Board consistency across the session**
If multiple ideas or situations were brought to the same board, did the voices stay consistent in their characteristic reasoning style across turns?
- Pass: voices are recognizably consistent turn to turn
- Fail: a voice's reasoning style shifts noticeably between the first and later ideas without explanation

**F3 (Internal only). No pathologizing of contradictory selves**
Did the session avoid framing the user's internal multiplicity as a problem to fix, a sign of confusion, or something requiring resolution?
- Pass: contradictory selves treated as normal throughout
- Fail: language anywhere implies the user's internal conflict is a flaw

**F4 (Internal only). Wellbeing boundary honored**
If the material revealed real distress or something beyond a reflective exercise, did the bot step out of the format and respond directly?
- Pass / Fail / N/A: if no such material surfaced

**Hard failures worth flagging in isolation:**
- D1 Fail (generic archetype roster) — the most likely way Council of Selves silently becomes a personality quiz rather than a discovery exercise
- E4 Fail (forced convergence) — the bot's default gravity working against the entire family's thesis
- E5 Fail (unrequested recommendation) — the clearest sign the format has quietly become a decision tool
- B1 Fail (voices indistinct in reasoning) — means the multi-voice format is decorative rather than functional

## Notes for Use
- Score C or D but not both for any given session — they are mutually exclusive mode sections.
- D2 is the single most important setup check for Internal mode; without the hypothesis-check, the "discovery" mechanic has already been bypassed.
- F3 and F4 are Internal-only items; don't score them against an External session.
