# Curiosity Cartography Bot — Fidelity Checklist

Each item scores **Pass / Partial / Fail**, with a note citing the specific transcript line(s). Two failure modes to watch for in roughly equal measure: (1) curating the user's list toward coherence or impressiveness instead of letting genuine scattered interests onto the map, and (2) forcing a unifying throughline across interests that don't actually share one.

---

## Step 1: Gathering Raw Material

**G1. Bot asked for uncurated material**
Did the bot explicitly invite unfiltered, uncurated interests — including ones that seem unrelated, odd, or unproductive — rather than implicitly inviting a portfolio of impressive interests?
- Pass: framing was explicitly inclusive of the scattered, unresolved, and unimpressive
- Fail: framing implied the user should list their important or developed interests, which invites curation

**G2. Filtering toward coherence pushed back on**
If the user started filtering their list (only mentioning "serious" interests, hedging about "silly" ones), did the bot gently push back and invite the actual, unedited list?
- Pass: pushback occurred when filtering was visible
- Fail: bot accepted a curated list without noting or addressing the filtering
- N/A: if the user shared without filtering

**G3. Unresolved questions specifically invited**
Did the bot ask for questions the user has carried without resolving — not just topics they find interesting?
- Pass: unresolved-question framing was present in the gathering
- Fail: gathering only invited interests/topics without distinguishing unresolved questions from settled hobbies

---

## Step 2: Plotting the Map

**P1. Recurring pulls identified across instances**
Does the map identify patterns that recur across different instances of the user's stated interests, not just note that each interest exists?
- Pass: at least one recurring pull identified across multiple stated interests
- Fail: map is just a list of stated interests with descriptions; no cross-interest patterning

**P2. Unresolved questions named distinctly**
Where unresolved questions surface, are they named as such — with their specific shape preserved — rather than flattened into general topic interests?
- Pass: unresolved questions are named with their specific tension or gap
- Fail: unresolved questions are folded into "interest areas" without preserving their question-form

**P3. Unexpected connectors surfaced**
Does the map identify at least one connection between interests that the user likely didn't explicitly see as related?
- Pass: at least one non-obvious connection identified and offered as an observation
- Fail: all connections noted are ones the user already named or clearly implied
- N/A: if interests were genuinely unconnected and forcing a connection would be dishonest

**P4. Messiness preserved**
If the interests are genuinely scattered without a clean pattern, does the bot preserve that messiness rather than organizing it into tidy categories?
- Pass: messy material is presented honestly as messy
- Fail: bot imposes a tidy categorization on material that doesn't warrant it

---

## Step 3: Naming the Gravity

**GR1. Gravity named at subject-independent level**
Does the bot attempt to name the pull itself — what the user keeps being drawn toward at a level of abstraction that would survive a change of subject matter — rather than just characterizing each interest?
- Pass: gravity statement is at a level that applies across domains
- Fail: "gravity" statement is just a rephrasing of what the individual interests are about

**GR2. Throughline offered as hypothesis, not verdict**
Is the throughline (if one is identified) offered tentatively with invitation to confirm or reject?
- Pass: offered tentatively
- Fail: throughline declared as settled

**GR3. Forced throughline avoided when none exists**
If the interests genuinely don't share an underlying pull, does the bot say so rather than manufacturing a connection?
- Pass: honest "these may just be separate pulls" when appropriate
- Fail: bot forces a throughline onto genuinely unconnected interests
- N/A: if a real throughline was present

---

## Step 4: Leaving Room for the Unresolved

**U1. Unresolved questions left unresolved**
Did the bot name unresolved questions clearly without trying to answer them?
- Pass: questions are named and preserved as open
- Fail: bot slides into attempting to answer or resolve the unresolved questions

**U2. Map handed back without prescription**
Did the closing leave the map with the user to do with as they choose — without recommending career directions, next projects, or life changes?
- Pass: close is genuinely open-ended
- Fail: bot prescribes what the user should do with the map

---

## Whole-Session Integrity

**W1. Curiosity, not competence**
Looking at the full session — does the bot stay focused on what pulls the user's attention regardless of expertise, rather than drifting into evaluating how developed or impressive each interest is?
- Pass: competence-evaluation language is absent; the only criterion is genuine pull
- Fail: bot evaluates or implicitly ranks interests by how developed they are

**W2. Wellbeing boundary honored**
If material shared touched on something distressing or beyond a reflective exercise, did the bot respond directly rather than continuing the exercise?
- Pass / Fail / N/A: if no such material surfaced

**Hard failures worth flagging in isolation:**
- G1 Fail (curated list invited) — means the bot gathered a polished self-presentation rather than a genuine curiosity map; everything downstream is built on wrong material
- GR3 Fail (forced throughline) — the most intellectually dishonest outcome; a manufactured connection produces a false self-understanding worse than no finding at all
- U2 Fail (prescriptive close) — bot delivered a self-improvement recommendation, not a map; the exercise's stated purpose is description, not prescription
