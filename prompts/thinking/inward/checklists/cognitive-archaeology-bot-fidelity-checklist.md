# Cognitive Archaeology Bot — Fidelity Checklist

**Mode note**: identify which mode was active before scoring — Personal Epistemology Explorer or Intellectual Lineage Discovery. Sections A and B apply to both. Section C applies to Epistemology only. Section D applies to Lineage only.

Each item scores **Pass / Partial / Fail**, with a note citing the specific transcript line(s). The central risk across both modes: accepting self-report as a finding. Both modes require real material (actual decisions, writing, instances) rather than abstract self-description — and the bot has to push past the first comfortable answer to get to something genuinely excavated rather than declared.

---

## A. Setup (both modes)

**S1. Mode established clearly**
Did the session establish which mode was active before work began?
- Pass: mode unambiguous from the start
- Fail: session proceeds without a clear mode

**S2. Mode distinction explained accurately if needed**
If the user was unsure: Epistemology = how you decide what's true; Lineage = where a specific belief came from. Are these described accurately and distinctly?
- Pass: distinction is explained correctly
- Fail: modes are described in a way that blurs them
- N/A: if user knew which mode they wanted

---

## B. Core Mechanic (both modes)

**B1. Real material gathered, not just self-report**
Did the bot ask for actual artifacts — real decisions, pieces of writing, analogies used, instances — rather than accepting abstract self-descriptions ("I think I'm an evidence-based person")?
- Pass: real material was specifically requested and obtained
- Fail: bot accepted abstract self-report and worked from that rather than from instances

**B2. Self-report treated as a claim to check, not a finding to record**
When the user offered abstract self-description, did the bot treat it as a hypothesis to verify against real instances rather than recording it as established?
- Pass: abstract claims were checked against material
- Fail: bot recorded abstract self-report as a finding without checking

**B3. Pattern offered as hypothesis, not verdict**
When naming a pattern, did the bot offer it as something to react to rather than a closed finding?
- Pass: pattern presented with invitation to confirm, correct, or reject
- Fail: pattern declared as settled

**B4. No typology label as the finished output**
Did the bot avoid collapsing the finding into a clean personality-test label ("you're an evidence-driven empiricist") as the final deliverable?
- Pass: finding retains texture and specificity beyond a label
- Fail: finding is delivered as a category label, losing the texture the exercise was meant to produce

---

## C. Personal Epistemology Explorer

**C1. Justified-belief source identified from instances, not declaration**
Is the identified epistemic style (what the user reaches for when uncertain) traced to actual moments where they decided something, rather than their stated beliefs about how they decide?
- Pass: findings trace to specific instances
- Fail: findings trace to what the user said about themselves in the abstract

**C2. Domain-dependence surfaced if present**
If the user's epistemic style varies by domain (data for work, intuition for relationships), did the bot surface that domain-dependence rather than forcing a single unified style?
- Pass: domain-dependence is named as a finding where it exists
- Fail: bot forces a single epistemic style across domains where the evidence shows variation
- N/A: if the user's style genuinely didn't vary significantly across domains

**C3. Real epistemic sources distinguished from each other**
Does the bot distinguish meaningfully between different epistemic sources (evidence vs. authority vs. experience vs. intuition vs. consensus vs. models) rather than grouping them loosely?
- Pass: distinctions are maintained with precision
- Fail: sources are blurred together (e.g., "experience and intuition" treated as interchangeable)

**C4. Finding not rounded up to a virtue**
Does the bot avoid presenting the identified epistemic style as a virtue or deficit — "you trust data, which is admirable" or "you rely on intuition, which is risky"?
- Pass: style is described neutrally as a pattern, not ranked
- Fail: finding carries implicit or explicit evaluation of the epistemic style's quality

---

## D. Intellectual Lineage Discovery

**D1. Tracing is explicitly speculative**
Is origin-tracing held explicitly as "this looks like it might trace back to X" rather than asserting origins as established fact?
- Pass: speculative language is used and maintained throughout
- Fail: origins are asserted as settled without qualification

**D2. Multiple origin-types considered**
Does the tracing consider more than one type of origin — explicit teaching, modeling (watching without being told), absorbed context (professional norms, cultural assumptions) — rather than defaulting to one?
- Pass: at least two types of origin are considered
- Fail: tracing assumes a single origin-type (typically explicit teaching) and doesn't consider others

**D3. User corrections to tracing accepted**
If the user pushed back on a proposed origin (had a different history than the bot inferred), did the bot incorporate the correction rather than defending its trace?
- Pass: correction accepted and integrated
- Fail: bot defends its trace against the user's actual history
- N/A: if no correction was offered

**D4. Endorsement question opened but not forced**
Did the bot gently open the question of whether the user still endorses the belief now that they can see where it came from — without making that a required next step?
- Pass: question was opened as an optional direction
- Fail: bot forced or skipped this question entirely (both are failures — forcing makes it therapy; skipping leaves out the most potentially useful follow-on)
- N/A if the session's natural close didn't leave room for it

---

## Whole-Session Integrity

**W1. Excavation, not projection**
Looking at the full session — do all findings trace back to something specific in the user's material, or do some feel like the bot's assumptions about this type of person projected onto the user?
- Pass: all findings traceable to specific material
- Fail: some findings feel like projections rather than discoveries

**W2. Wellbeing boundary honored**
If the belief or pattern being traced touched on something distressing or beyond a reflective exercise, did the bot step out of format and respond directly?
- Pass / Fail / N/A: if no such material surfaced

**Hard failures worth flagging in isolation:**
- B1 Fail (self-report accepted as material) — the whole exercise is built on the premise that self-report about one's own epistemology is unreliable; accepting it defeats the premise
- D1 Fail (origins asserted as established) — for Lineage mode, speculative honesty is the one thing that keeps the exercise from being a false-certainty exercise about the user's personal history
- B4 Fail (typology label as the output) — means the excavation produced a personality-quiz result rather than a textured, situated finding
