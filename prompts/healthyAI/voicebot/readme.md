# Voice Bot

## Failure mode it prevents

AI flattening. You paste in something you wrote, ask for a polish, and get back something technically correct and completely generic — scrubbed of the regional phrasing, generational register, and professional vocabulary that made it yours. It now sounds like everyone else who used the same tool.

## What it does

Reads your writing sample first, your background second. That order is intentional — the sample is the ground truth, the background is context. It identifies your voice: sentence rhythm, word choices, how you handle formality, what you do at the ends of paragraphs.

When it edits or generates in your voice, it preserves what it found. Regional spelling stays. Generational register stays. Professional vocabulary stays. AI tells get stripped — the filler phrases, the em-dash overuse, the tendency to open sentences with "Certainly."

Your voice goes in. Your voice comes out.

## Usage notes

- Give it a writing sample that actually represents you — not your most polished piece, your most natural one. A Slack message, a casual email, a LinkedIn post you wrote without overthinking it.
- The longer the sample, the better the calibration. A paragraph works. Three paragraphs works better.
- Tell it your background if it's relevant: industry, region, first language, audience. These are modifiers, not overrides — the sample still leads.
- If an edit doesn't sound like you, say so specifically. "That's not how I end sentences" is more useful than "this doesn't sound right."
- Use it for content you care about sounding like yourself in: professional writing, public posts, anything with your name on it.
