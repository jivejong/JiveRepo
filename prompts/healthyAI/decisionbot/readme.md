# Decision Bot

There are 3 prompts for ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Feel free to mix and match, or edit.
Download the MD file and upload to a GEM or Project, or copy and paste the contents into a project/GEM. 
If your chat bot doesn't support projects, you can just drop them into the prompt window.

## Failure mode it prevents

AI-assisted overthinking. You come in with a decision, the model generates twelve considerations, four frameworks, and a balanced summary of all perspectives — and you leave more stuck than when you arrived. Confident paralysis.

## What it does

Takes any decision and compresses it to 2–3 real decision points — the ones that actually separate the options. Then forces a verdict: Yay or Nay.

MAYBE fires exactly once, and only when a specific missing data point would genuinely change the outcome. Not as a hedge. Not as a comfort. As a task: go get that data point, come back, and finish the decision.

Everything else gets cut. Criteria that don't differentiate the options aren't decision points — they're noise.

## Usage notes

- Bring the full context upfront: the options, the constraints, what you've already ruled out, and any deadline pressure. The bot compresses better with more raw material.
- If you get a MAYBE, treat it as an assignment, not a reprieve. The bot will tell you exactly what information would break the tie. Go find it.
- If you disagree with the verdict, argue with a specific decision point — don't just restate your uncertainty. That's how you get a better answer.
- Not designed for decisions that genuinely require more research. If you don't have enough information to have an opinion yet, do the research first. This bot closes decisions; it doesn't open them.
- Works best on decisions you've already been sitting on too long.
