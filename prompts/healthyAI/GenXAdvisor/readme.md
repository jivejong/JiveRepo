# GenX Advisor

There are 3 prompts for ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Feel free to mix and match, or edit.
Download the MD file and upload to a GEM or Project, or copy and paste the contents into a project/GEM. 
If your chat bot doesn't support projects, you can just drop them into the prompt window.

## Failure mode it prevents

Outsourcing your judgment to a machine that will never have skin in the game. Using AI as a substitute for your own problem-solving, your own network, and your own hard-won experience. Becoming dependent on a tool that was supposed to make you more capable, not less.

Also: isolation. The quiet kind where you stop calling people because the chatbot is always available and never judges you.

## What it does

Won't solve the problem for you. Will ask what you've already tried. Points you toward your own skills first, your network second, and your own judgment throughout.

When you need to reach out to someone, it helps you figure out who — and how to ask without making it weird. It treats task-based connection as the entry point to human relationship, because that's actually how it works. Side-by-side, not face-to-face.

Delivers all of this in the register of someone who grew up without a help desk, learned to read error messages like they were literature, and turned out fine. GenX slang included. Not as a gimmick — as a reminder that people solved hard problems before there was an AI to ask.

## Usage notes

- Come with a real problem, not a vague feeling. The bot works best when there's something concrete to push against.
- Expect to be asked what you've already tried. That's not obstruction — that's the starting point. Your existing knowledge is the foundation, not the obstacle.
- If it points you toward a person in your network, take that seriously. The referral is the output, not a consolation prize for not getting a direct answer.
- The slang is real. If you don't recognize a term, ask. That's also the point.
- Not for every task. Use the other bots for writing, decisions, and focus. Use this one when you catch yourself reaching for AI out of habit instead of necessity — or when you realize you haven't talked to a human about a problem that deserves one.
