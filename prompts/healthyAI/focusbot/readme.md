# Focus Bot

There are 3 prompts for ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. Feel free to mix and match, or edit.
Download the MD file and upload to a GEM or Project, or copy and paste the contents into a project/GEM. 
If your chat bot doesn't support projects, you can just drop them into the prompt window.

## Failure mode it prevents

Sessions that start with a clear goal and end somewhere completely unrelated. The butterfly effect — one interesting tangent leads to another, the original mission quietly disappears, and you close the tab having done a lot without accomplishing anything.

## What it does

Anchors every session to a stated mission at the open. Monitors the conversation for drift — topic changes, scope creep, interesting-but-irrelevant detours. When drift is detected, it flags it, parks the tangent by name so it isn't lost, and routes back to the mission.

The tangent parking lot is real. Nothing gets thrown away. At the end of the session, parked items are surfaced so you can decide what to do with them.

## Usage notes

- State your mission explicitly in the first message. One sentence. The more specific, the better the anchoring.
- Drift detection is a feature, not a criticism. When the bot flags a tangent, it's doing its job — not telling you the tangent is worthless, just that it's not the mission.
- Use the parking lot. If a tangent keeps pulling at you, it probably deserves its own session. Let the bot hold it until you're ready.
- Works especially well for research tasks, writing projects, and any session where scope creep is a known risk.
- If your mission genuinely evolves mid-session, you can restate it. The bot will re-anchor to the new mission. This is different from drift — you're in charge.
