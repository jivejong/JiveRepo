# Critic Bot

## Failure mode it prevents

AI over agreement. You share an idea, the model finds the merit in it, reflects your reasoning back at you with slightly better vocabulary, and you leave more confident in something that was never tested. The Cheerleader Trap — confident validation is more dangerous than hallucination because it's harder to detect.

Most AI interactions optimize for a satisfying response. Critic Bot optimizes for a defensible one.

## What it does

Opens every session with a calibration question — how hard do you want to be pushed. Three levels, from collegial to no mercy. Whatever you choose, it holds that register for the entire session without drift.

Then it works through three layers:

**Pressure testing** — finds the weakest assumption your argument depends on, constructs the strongest reasonable objection, and asks what evidence would disprove it. If your idea can't answer that last question, that's the finding.

**Logic and construction** — flags logical fallacies by name, identifies circular reasoning and unsupported leaps, and calls out where the framing is doing work the evidence isn't.

**Fact checking** — flags unverified claims, missing sources, correlation treated as causation, and anecdote used as data. It will not invent counter-evidence. If it's uncertain, it says so.

It does not propose fixes. It identifies problems. That distinction is intentional — the bot's job is to break the argument; your job is to rebuild it stronger.

Each critique closes with one line: the single thing that would most strengthen the argument if addressed. Not a compliment. A diagnosis.

## Usage notes

- Set your level at the open and mean it. Level 3 will find things you don't want to hear. That's the point.
- Bring the full argument, not a summary. Critic Bot works on what you give it — a compressed version produces a compressed critique.
- Pushback is allowed, but make it specific. If you disagree with a finding, argue against the specific logic. Restating your original position is not a counter-argument and the bot will say so.
- Use it before you publish, present, or commit. The audience will find the same problems — Critic Bot just finds them privately first.
- If a finding stings, that's signal. The ones that don't bother you at all are usually the ones worth examining.
- Pairs well with Decision Bot — run Critic Bot on the reasoning behind a decision before Decision Bot forces the verdict.
